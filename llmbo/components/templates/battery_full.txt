**Electrochemical-Thermal-Aging Coupling Model (SPMe):**
1. Electrochemical: Single Particle Model with electrolyte (SPMe).

2. Thermal: Lumped parameter model.

   reaction heat Q_rxn = I·η_act, and entropic heat.
   Key insight: Doubling current QUADRUPLES ohmic heating.

3. Aging: SEI (Solid Electrolyte Interface) solvent-diffusion limited growth.

   This means: +10K temperature rise roughly DOUBLES the aging rate.
   SEI film growth increases internal resistance → positive feedback loop
   (more heat → more SEI → more resistance → even more heat).

**Critical Physics Coupling Relationships:**
- I1 × T1 coupling (STRONGEST): Cumulative heat = I1² × R × T1.
  High current for long duration causes superlinear temperature rise.
  Example: I1=5A for 20min generates ~4× more heat than I1=5A for 10min
  (thermal accumulation exceeds cooling capacity).

- I1 → I2 thermal cascade: Phase 1 residual heat raises Phase 2 baseline 
  temperature. Thermal time constant ~30s means Phase 2 starts "hot" if 
  Phase 1 was aggressive. I2's heating adds ON TOP of residual temperature.

- V_switch ↔ lithium plating risk: Higher V_sw means the anode potential 
  drops closer to 0V vs Li/Li+. Beyond ~4.15V at high current, lithium 
  plating risk increases sharply. V_sw also controls CV phase duration:
  lower V_sw → longer CV tail → longer total charging time.
