## 3. Algorithmic Directives (Algorithm Mechanism & Task Instructions)

**Task:**
Construct three 3x3 Symmetric Positive Semi-Definite Coupling Matrices (W_time, W_temp, W_aging) where w_{ij} represents the interaction strength between parameter i and j for each objective.

**Reasoning Steps (Chain of Thought):**

1. **I1 ↔ SOC1 (Current × Switch Point):**
   Heat accumulation is the integral of power over Phase 1 duration.
   I1 controls Power, SOC1 controls duration → their joint effect on temperature
   is multiplicative (superlinear interaction).

2. **I1 ↔ I2 (Thermal Cascade):**
   Phase 1 residual heat affects Phase 2 starting temperature.
   High I1 creates elevated baseline for I2's heating effects.
   Coupling occurs through thermal inertia (time constant ~30s).

3. **SOC1 ↔ I2 (Switch Point × Phase 2 Current):**
   Higher SOC1 means shorter Phase 2, making I2 impact more critical.
   Aging is particularly sensitive to this interaction (lithium plating risk).

**Matrix Construction Rules:**
- Diagonal elements w_ii = 1.0 (self-coupling is maximum)
- Off-diagonal values in [0, 1] (symmetric)
- Higher values indicate stronger interaction between parameters

**Objective-Specific Coupling:**
- W_time: Dominated by I1 and I2 magnitudes (direct time contribution)
- W_temp: I1 dominant (Joule heating at low SOC), I1-SOC1 interaction important
- W_aging: SOC1-I2 interaction critical (lithium plating at high SOC)
